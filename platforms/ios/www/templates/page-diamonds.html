The cut determines a diamond's shape and brilliance.

A well-cut diamond reflects light from one facet to another, then disperse it through the top of the diamond.

Do not confuse the cut of a diamond with its shape. Diamonds can be cut into a number of shapes according to the natural rough stone. Pick the right shape.

Common shapes are Round, Brilliant and Emerald Cut.

Popular less common shapes include Marquise, Oval, Pear, Princess and Heart, etc.

Refers to the purity of a diamond.

There are 2 types of flaws – External surface blemishes (present during the cutting process, often at the girdle). Internal inclusions

High quality diamonds have no or few inclusions and are rare and highly priced.

A 10x magnifying glass or loupe and a trained eye will be able to determine a diamond’s clarity.

VS1-VS2

Very Slightly Included 1 and 2. Minute inclusions seen only with difficulty under 10x magnification. Easy to find in a wide range of prices (for a one-carat round brilliant diamond, prices start around $3,750).

SI1-SI2

Slightly Included 1 and 2. Minute inclusions are visible under 10x magnification, and may be visible by the unaided eye. Easy to find in a wide range of prices (for a one-carat round brilliant diamond, prices start around $3,500).

I1-I2-I3

Inclusions 1, 2, and 3. Inclusions visible under 10x magnification as well as to the human eye. We do not recommend buying diamonds in any of these grades.

Diamonds are weighed in Carats.

One carat is divided into 100 "points," so that a diamond of 75 points weights .75 ct.

The carat-weight of a diamond is the easiest measurement to determine.

Most importantly, two diamonds can be of equal carat-weight, but their value can differ greatly due to their cut, color, and clarity.

Set a carat weight minimum based on the recipient's preferences. A carat weight difference of 10% or less will not be noticeable visually.

You may choose a smaller carat diamond in favor of a higher quality diamond.

Scratch the diamond against glass. If the glass is scraped or scratched, the diamond is real.

Place the diamond over a newspaper or other printed text — real diamonds refract light so much that you wouldn't be able to see any lines, circles or full letters coming through.

Fog Test. Diamonds are good at conducting heat, so the fog your breath creates should clear up almost instantly. However, a synthetic rock called moissanite can pass this test.

Weight Test. If your stone is a simulated diamond or cubic zirconia (CZ), it will be about 50 percent heavier than a real diamond of equal size.
